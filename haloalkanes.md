## **2. Basics of Organic Chemistry (Class 11th Recap)**

### **A. Representation of Organic Compounds**
Organic compounds can be represented in four main ways:

1.  **Molecular Formula:**
    *   Shows the exact number of atoms present in the compound.
    *   *Examples:*
        *   Methane: $CH_4$
        *   Butane: $C_4H_{10}$
        *   Glucose: $C_6H_{12}O_6$

2.  **Expanded Structural Formula (ESF):**
    *   All chemical bonds between atoms are represented clearly.
    *   *Example:* In Propane, every C-H and C-C bond is drawn out.

3.  **Condensed Structural Formula (CSF):**
    *   Only Carbon-Carbon (C-C) bonds are shown (sometimes even those are omitted), and Hydrogen atoms are written next to the Carbon they are attached to.
    *   *Example:* $H_3C-CH_2-CH_2-CH_3$

4.  **Bond Line Notation (BLN):**
    *   Carbon atoms are represented by the vertices and ends of zig-zag lines.
    *   Hydrogen atoms attached to carbon are implied to satisfy carbon's valency (4).
    *   *Examples:*
        *   Propane: A simple "mountain" shape ($\wedge$).
        *   Cyclobutane: A square.

### **C. Hybridisation**

The hybridisation of a carbon atom can be determined by counting the number of Sigma ($\sigma$) bonds and Lone Pairs (LP).

**Rule:**
$$\text{Hybridisation Number (Z)} = \text{Number of } \sigma \text{ bonds} + \text{Lone Pairs}$$

| Hybridisation Number | Type | S-Character | p-Character | Geometry |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 4 | $sp^3$ | 25% ($\frac{1}{4}$) | 75% | Tetrahedral |
| 3 | $sp^2$ | 33.3% ($\frac{1}{3}$) | 66.7% | Trigonal Planar |
| 2 | $sp$ | 50% ($\frac{1}{2}$) | 50% | Linear |

**Bond Counting Tips:**
*   **Single Bond:** $1 \sigma$
*   **Double Bond:** $1 \sigma + 1 \pi$
*   **Triple Bond:** $1 \sigma + 2 \pi$
*   **Note:** Pi ($\pi$) bonds do not participate in hybridisation.

**Question:** A $sp^3$ hybrid orbital contains:
*   **Answer:** $1/4$ s-character (25%).

### **D. Degree of Carbon and Hydrogen**

The degree describes the connectivity of carbon atoms within a molecule.

#### **1. Degree of Carbon ($^\circ C$)**
*   **Primary Carbon ($1^\circ$):** Carbon connected to only **one** other carbon atom.
    *   *Special Case:* "Superprimary Carbon" (e.g., $CH_4$ or $CH_3-X$ where C is attached to no other carbons).
*   **Secondary Carbon ($2^\circ$):** Carbon connected to **two** other carbon atoms.
*   **Tertiary Carbon ($3^\circ$):** Carbon connected to **three** other carbon atoms.
*   **Quaternary Carbon ($4^\circ$):** Carbon connected to **four** other carbon atoms.

#### **2. Degree of Hydrogen ($^\circ H$)**
The degree of hydrogen is determined by the carbon it is attached to.
*   **$1^\circ$ Hydrogen:** Attached to a $1^\circ$ Carbon.
*   **$2^\circ$ Hydrogen:** Attached to a $2^\circ$ Carbon.
*   **$3^\circ$ Hydrogen:** Attached to a $3^\circ$ Carbon.
*   **Note:** There is **no** $4^\circ$ Hydrogen because a quaternary carbon has satisfied all 4 valencies with carbon atoms and cannot hold a hydrogen.

### **E. Practice Question on Degrees**

**Q: Calculate the number of $1^\circ, 2^\circ, 3^\circ, 4^\circ$ Carbons and Hydrogens in the following structure:**
$$CH_3-CH_2-C(CH_3)_2-CH_2-CH(CH_3)-CH_3$$

**Analysis:**
1.  **Carbons:**
    *   **$1^\circ$ C:** 5 carbons (The terminal methyl groups).
    *   **$2^\circ$ C:** 2 carbons (The $-CH_2-$ groups).
    *   **$3^\circ$ C:** 1 carbon (The $CH$ group branching off).
    *   **$4^\circ$ C:** 1 carbon (The central $C$ with two methyls attached).

2.  **Hydrogens:**
    *   **$1^\circ$ H:** $5 \times 3 = 15$
    *   **$2^\circ$ H:** $2 \times 2 = 4$
    *   **$3^\circ$ H:** $1 \times 1 = 1$

**Final Answer:**
*   **C:** $5, 2, 1, 1$
*   **H:** $15, 4, 1$

### **F. Degree of Alkyl Halide (Haloalkane)**

Classification depends on the carbon to which the Halogen ($X = F, Cl, Br, I$) is attached.

1.  **Primary Alkyl Halide ($1^\circ$):**
    *   Halogen is attached to a **$1^\circ$ Carbon**.
    *   *Structure:* $R-CH_2-X$
2.  **Secondary Alkyl Halide ($2^\circ$):**
    *   Halogen is attached to a **$2^\circ$ Carbon**.
    *   *Structure:* $R_2CH-X$
3.  **Tertiary Alkyl Halide ($3^\circ$):**
    *   Halogen is attached to a **$3^\circ$ Carbon**.
    *   *Structure:* $R_3C-X$

**Question:** Which of the following is a $1^\circ$ alkyl halide?
1.  $R-CH_2-X$ (Correct Answer)
2.  $R_2CH-X$ ($2^\circ$)
3.  $R_3C-X$ ($3^\circ$)
4.  $R-H$ (Alkane, not halide)

### **G. IUPAC Nomenclature Basics**

The standard formula for naming organic compounds:

$$ \text{2}^\circ \text{ Prefix} + \text{1}^\circ \text{ Prefix} + \text{Word Root} + \text{1}^\circ \text{ Suffix} + \text{2}^\circ \text{ Suffix} $$

1.  **$2^\circ$ Prefix:** Substituents / Juniormost functional groups / Side chains.
    *   *Examples:* Halo ($F, Cl, Br, I$), Nitro ($-NO_2$), Alkyl ($-R$), Alkoxy ($-OR$).
2.  **$1^\circ$ Prefix:** Used for cyclic compounds.
    *   *Example:* "Cyclo".
3.  **Word Root:** Indicates the number of carbons in the main (parent) chain.
    *   $C_1$: Meth, $C_2$: Eth, $C_3$: Prop, $C_4$: But, $C_5$: Pent, $C_6$: Hex, $C_7$: Hept, $C_8$: Oct, $C_9$: Non, $C_{10}$: Dec.
4.  **$1^\circ$ Suffix:** Type of bond between Carbon atoms (Saturation).
    *   Single bond ($C-C$): **-ane**
    *   Double bond ($C=C$): **-ene**
    *   Triple bond ($C \equiv C$): **-yne**
5.  **$2^\circ$ Suffix:** Main (Senior) Functional Group.
    *   *Examples:* -ol (Alcohol), -al (Aldehyde), -oic acid (Carboxylic acid).

# **Haloalkanes & Haloarenes – Lecture 02**

## **1. Classification by Degree (Recap & Application)**

The classification of alkyl halides depends on the nature of the carbon atom to which the halogen ($X$) is attached.

*   **Primary ($1^\circ$) Alkyl Halide:** Halogen attached to a $1^\circ$ Carbon.
*   **Secondary ($2^\circ$) Alkyl Halide:** Halogen attached to a $2^\circ$ Carbon.
*   **Tertiary ($3^\circ$) Alkyl Halide:** Halogen attached to a $3^\circ$ Carbon.

### **Important Question: C$_4$H$_9$X**
**Q:** $C_4H_9X$ represents which type of alkyl halide?
1.  Pri-alkyl halide
2.  Sec-alkyl halide
3.  Ter-alkyl halide
4.  **Primary, sec and tertiary alkyl halide (Correct Answer)**

**Explanation:** The formula $C_4H_9X$ (Butyl halide) can exist as isomers:
*   **$1^\circ$:** $CH_3-CH_2-CH_2-CH_2-X$ (n-butyl halide) or Isobutyl halide.
*   **$2^\circ$:** $CH_3-CH_2-CH(X)-CH_3$ (sec-butyl halide).
*   **$3^\circ$:** $(CH_3)_3C-X$ (tert-butyl halide).
*   *Conclusion:* It can be any of the three depending on the structure.

## **2. IUPAC Nomenclature of Haloalkanes**

### **The Naming Formula**
$$ \text{2}^\circ \text{ Prefix} + \text{1}^\circ \text{ Prefix} + \text{Word Root} + \text{1}^\circ \text{ Suffix} + \text{2}^\circ \text{ Suffix} $$

*   **$2^\circ$ Prefix:** Substituents (Bromo, Chloro, Fluoro, Iodo, Methyl, Ethyl, etc.). *Arranged Alphabetically.*
*   **Word Root:** Carbon chain length (Meth, Eth, Prop, But...).
*   **$1^\circ$ Suffix:** Saturation (ane, ene, yne).

### **Basic Examples**
1.  **$CH_3-CH_2-CH_3$:** Propane.
2.  **$CH_3-CH(CH_3)-CH_3$:** 2-Methylpropane.
3.  **$CH_3-CH_2-CH_2-Br$:**
    *   Prefix: 1-Bromo
    *   Root: Prop
    *   Suffix: ane
    *   **Name:** 1-Bromopropane.
4.  **$CH_3-CH(CH_3)-CH_2Br$:**
    *   Numbering starts from the carbon attached to Br (Lowest Locant Rule).
    *   At C-1: Bromo, At C-2: Methyl.
    *   Alphabetical order: Bromo comes before Methyl.
    *   **Name:** 1-Bromo-2-methylpropane.
  




### **Tricky Examples (Chain Selection)**
**Q:** Write the IUPAC name for: $CH_3-CH(C_2H_5)-CH_2-Br$
*   *Common Mistake:* Selecting the straight horizontal chain (Propane).
*   *Correction:* Expand the Ethyl group ($C_2H_5 \rightarrow -CH_2-CH_3$). The longest chain containing the Halogen is 4 carbons long (Butane).
*   **Numbering:** Start from $CH_2Br$ (Carbon 1).
*   **Substituent:** Methyl group at Carbon 2.
*   **Name:** **1-Bromo-2-methylbutane**.






**Q:** $(CH_3)_2CH-CH_2-CH_2Br$
*   **Structure:** $CH_3-CH(CH_3)-CH_2-CH_2-Br$
*   **Numbering:** Right to left (Br gets 1).
*   **Name:** **1-Bromo-3-methylbutane**.

## **3. IUPAC Nomenclature for Alkenes (Double Bonds)**

When a double bond and a halogen are both present, the **Double Bond gets priority** over the Halogen substituent for numbering.

**Rule:** $\text{Multiple Bond} > \text{Prefix (Halogen)}$

### **Examples:**
1.  **$CH_3-CH=CH-CH(Br)-CH_3$**
    *   Numbering starts from Left (Double bond gets 2). If we started from right, double bond gets 3.
    *   **Name:** **4-Bromopent-2-ene**.

2.  **$H_2C=C(CH_3)-CH(Br)-CH_3$**
    *   Numbering starts from Left (Double bond gets 1).
    *   Substituents: 2-Methyl, 3-Bromo.
    *   Alphabetical: Bromo first.
    *   **Name:** **3-Bromo-2-methylbut-1-ene**.

3.  **$CH_3-C(CH_3)=CH-CH_2-Br$**
    *   Numbering starts from Left (Double bond gets 2). From right, double bond gets 2 also.
    *   Tie-breaker: Look at lowest set of locants.
        *   Left to Right: Methyl at 2, Alkene at 2, Bromo at 4. (2, 4)
        *   Right to Left: Bromo at 1, Alkene at 2, Methyl at 3. (1, 3) $\rightarrow$ **Preferred path.**
    *   *Correction from Slide 19 (Check visual):* In slide 19 (example v), the structure is $CH_3-CH=CH-CH_2Br$. Numbering is from Right to Left to give Br position 1 and Alkene position 2?
        *   *Wait, Strict IUPAC Rule:* Double bond gets lower number preference over substituent. If double bond gets same number from both sides, *then* give preference to substituent.
        *   *Let's look at Slide 19 (v):* Structure: $CH_3-CH=CH-CH_2Br$.
            *   Left to Right: Alkene at 2, Br at 4.
            *   Right to Left: Alkene at 2, Br at 1.
            *   Since Alkene is at 2 in both cases, we choose Right to Left.
            *   **Name:** **1-Bromobut-2-ene**.

4.  **Slide 20 Example:** $CH_3-C(CH_3)=C(H)-CH_2Br$
    *   Right to Left numbering.
    *   **Name:** **1-Bromo-3-methylbut-2-ene**. (Note: Slide 19 image iv label seems to match this logic).
    *   *Slide 20 Specific:* $H_2C=C(CH_3)-CH(Br)-H$ isn't the image.

        *   Structure: $H_2C=C(CH_3)-CH(Br)-H$ ... No, the image is Propene derivative.

## **4. Common Naming System**

Common names rely on the total number of carbons and the structural arrangement.

### **Prefixes:**
1.  **n- (normal):** Straight chain.
    *   *Ex:* n-hexane, n-pentane.
2.  **iso-:** A methyl group attached to the **second last** carbon of the chain.
    *   *Ex:* Isopentyl group ($CH_3-CH(CH_3)-CH_2-CH_2-$).
    *   *Special Case:* **Isobutyl** ($CH_3-CH(CH_3)-CH_2-$). Note that Isobutyl is a **Primary ($1^\circ$)** alkyl group because the valency is on a $1^\circ$ carbon.
    *   *Exception:* **Isopropyl** ($CH_3-CH-CH_3$ with valency on middle C). Isopropyl is a **Secondary ($2^\circ$)** alkyl group.
3.  **neo-:** Two methyl groups on the second last carbon (Quaternary carbon involved).
    *   *Ex:* Neopentyl ($(CH_3)_3C-CH_2$). Neopentyl is a **Primary ($1^\circ$)** alkyl group.

4.  **sec- (secondary):** The functional group is attached to a secondary carbon.
    *   *Ex:* sec-butyl chloride ($CH_3-CH_2-CH(Cl)-CH_3$).
    *   This is a **Secondary ($2^\circ$)** halide.

5.  **tert- (tertiary):** The functional group is attached to a tertiary carbon.
    *   *Ex:* tert-butyl chloride ($(CH_3)_3C-Cl$).
    *   This is a **Tertiary ($3^\circ$)** halide.

### **Practice Question: Classification**
**Q: Which of the following is a primary ($1^\circ$) halide?**
1.  iso-Propyl iodide ($2^\circ$)
2.  sec-Butyl iodide ($2^\circ$)
3.  tert-Butyl bromide ($3^\circ$)
4.  **neo-Hexyl chloride ($1^\circ$)** $\rightarrow$ **Correct.**
    *   *Reason:* In neo-hexyl, the structure ends with $-C(CH_3)_2-CH_2-Cl$. The Cl is on a $CH_2$, making it primary.

## **5. Difference: Haloalkane vs Haloarene**

| Feature | Haloalkane (Alkyl Halide) | Haloarene (Aryl Halide) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Structure** | Halogen attached to Aliphatic Hydrocarbon (Open chain). | Halogen attached to Aromatic Hydrocarbon (Benzene ring). |
| **Hybridization** | Halogen attached to **$sp^3$** Hybridised Carbon. | Halogen attached to **$sp^2$** Hybridised Carbon. |
| **Example** | $CH_3-CH_2-X$ | Chlorobenzene ($C_6H_5Cl$) |
